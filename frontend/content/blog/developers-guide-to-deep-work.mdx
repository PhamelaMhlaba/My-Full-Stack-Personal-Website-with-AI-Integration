# Why I'm Starting This Blog: Building Technology That Creates Opportunity

Hi, I'm **Phamela Mhlaba**, and welcome.

I'm a Full Stack AI Engineer. This blog is where I will be documenting everything I'm building, learning, experimenting with, and discovering as I grow in technology.

But this is not just another tech blog.

Yes, I will write about software engineering, AI, coding, architecture, debugging, and the lessons that come with building real applications. But underneath all of that is something much bigger.

This blog exists because I believe technology should create opportunity, not just for those who already have access, but especially for those who don't.

And that's a mission that has become deeply personal.

---

# My Journey Into Software Engineering

If you had asked me a few years ago whether I would become a Software Engineer, my answer probably would have been no.

Like many people, I viewed software engineering as something reserved for "geniuses", people who had been coding since childhood or who simply possessed a natural talent that I didn't think I had.

When Artificial Intelligence began transforming industries across the world, I knew one thing for certain:

I needed to equip myself with skills that would remain valuable in an AI-driven economy.

I didn't want to watch the future happen.

I wanted to help build it and remain relevant. 

That decision led me to pivot into Software Engineering.

To be honest, I was intimidated.

The terminology was unfamiliar.

The code looked impossible to understand.

There were moments when opening my editor felt overwhelming.

Yet something unexpected happened during my first two weeks of learning.

Instead of becoming discouraged, I became fascinated.

Every bug felt like a puzzle.

Every project challenged me to think differently.

Every small breakthrough made me curious enough to keep going.

That was the moment I knew I had found the career I wanted to pursue.

I was not just learning to code.

I was learning how to solve problems.

And I absolutely loved it.

# The Mindset That Keeps Me Going

Software engineering is hard.

There are days when nothing works.

There are bugs that refuse to disappear.

There are concepts that take hours or sometimes days to fully understand.

This industry quickly teaches you that motivation is not enough.

What matters is resilience and the discilpine to show up consitently even on days you do not feel like it 

One thing about me is that I don't quit easily. I AM NOT A QUITTER

When I don't understand something, I don't tell myself I'm not smart enough.

I don't disqualify myself.

I stay with the problem.

I ask questions.

I research.

I rebuild.

I fail.

I try again.

And I continue until I understand it.

I have never liked feeling defeated by a problem.

Over time, I have realised that this mindset has become one of my greatest strengths in my career.

Technology changes constantly.

New frameworks appear.

AI evolves almost daily.

No one can know everything.

But if you are willing to keep learning, you are never truly behind.

I believe a growth mindset is one of the most valuable skills any Developer can possess.

# The Bigger Problem I Can't Ignore

As I continue growing in this industry, I have become increasingly aware of something that concerns me.

We are living through one of the biggest technological shifts in history.

Artificial Intelligence is changing how we work, how we learn, how businesses operate, and how software is built.

Yet many young people, especially learners attending South African public schools are not being equipped with the digital skills they will need to thrive in this new world.

Our future innovators are entering adulthood without exposure to AI literacy, or even an understanding of the career opportunities technology offers.

The gap is not simply about access to devices or internet connectivity.

It's about exposure.

It's about mentorship.

It's about confidence.

It's about helping young people believe they belong in spaces they have never been invited into.

Our education system has not yet fully caught up with the pace at which technology is evolving.

And if we don't address that gap now, we risk leaving an entire generation behind.

That thought stays with me.

Because somewhere in South Africa today is a learner who could become an incredible software engineer, AI researcher, entrepreneur, or innovator, but may never discover that potential simply because nobody introduced them to the possibilities.

That should not happen under my watch. As an active citizen, I ask myself, what can I do with the knowledge I currently have.

# Why EdTech Matters to Me

This is why I'm so passionate about Educational Technology (EdTech).

I don't simply want to build software.

I want to build opportunity.

My long-term vision is to contribute meaningfully to improving digital education across South Africa, particularly in public schools.

I want to step into classrooms.

I want to teach Software Engineering.

I want to introduce learners to Artificial Intelligence in a way that removes fear and replaces it with curiosity.

I want to show students that technology isn't reserved for a select few.

It's something they can learn.

It's something they can build.

It's something they can use to transform their own lives and the communities around them.

I believe closing South Africa's digital skills gap requires more than infrastructure.

It requires people who are willing to teach, mentor, encourage, and create pathways for others.

If I can become one of those people, then every challenge I've faced learning this field will have been worth it.

* **Full Stack Software Engineering** - architecture, frontend development, backend systems, APIs, databases, authentication, deployment, and lessons learned while building real-world applications.
