Hi, I'm Phamela Mhlaba - a Full Stack AI Developer, and this is where I will be sharing everything I'm building, learning, and thinking about in tech.

I work across the stack - frontend, backend, and increasingly, AI integration, because I have  always been drawn to understanding how things connect end to end. But if you ask me what actually drives me, it's not the code itself. It's what the code can *do* for people who never had access to it.

## Where this started

I did not  get into software engineering just to build products. I got into it because I love teaching -  breaking complex ideas down until they click for someone, and watching that moment happen. That's the part of this work I care about most.

And the more time I spend in this industry, the more I notice a gap that keeps showing up: a lot of people - especially students coming up through South African public schools,  simply are not  being equipped with digital skills, let alone AI literacy. The curriculum has not caught up to where the world already is. Meanwhile, AI is reshaping how software gets built, how problems get solved, and how careers get started and most people still don't know how to use it effectively, let alone see themselves in this industry at all.

That gap is personal to me. And I don't think it closes by accident.

## What I'm building toward

My goal is not just to build in EdTech, it is to actually step into classrooms. I want to teach Software Engineering directly, go into public schools, and show students that this space is something they can genuinely belong to. Not as an abstract "coding is cool" pitch, but real, practical skills: how to think like an engineer, how to use AI as a tool rather than a mystery box, and how to see a career path that maybe nobody around them has ever pointed to before.

I believe closing South Africa's digital skills gap is not just about access to laptops or Wi-Fi ,  it's about mentorship, exposure, and someone showing up who looks like you, telling you this is possible.

## What you will find on this blog

This space is going to be a mix of:

- **Technical deep dives** - the real problems I run into building full stack AI applications, and how I solve them
- **AI literacy** - practical, no-fluff guidance on actually using AI tools effectively, not just talking about them
- **EdTech and education** - my thinking (and eventually, my work) on bridging the digital skills gap in South African schools
- **Behind-the-scenes** - what it actually looks like building in public as a solo developer

## Why I'm doing this in public

I'm sharing this journey openly because I think visibility matters. If a student stumbles across this blog one day and sees someone who looks like them, building things, teaching, and talking honestly about the gaps in this industry ,  maybe that's the nudge they needed to believe they belong here too.

That's the impact I'm working toward. Thanks for being here at the start of it.

— Phamela Mhlaba
